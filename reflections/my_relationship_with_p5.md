# My Relationship With p5.js

## Discovering the Ecosystem

I discovered the p5.js ecosystem while searching for open source organizations that use JavaScript as part of their technology stack. I was particularly interested in projects that had active communities and were welcoming to beginners.

During this search, I came across the Processing Foundation and the p5.js community. What stood out to me was the openness of the community and the emphasis on supporting learners and creators who are exploring programming for the first time.

The more I explored the ecosystem, the more it became clear that the community was not only active but also genuinely supportive of newcomers. This made it an environment where learning and contributing felt approachable.

## Why I Code Art

For me, coding art is a way to experiment with ideas and experiences visually.

The sketches I create are often inspired by things I have experienced, observed, or enjoyed interacting with in real life. Instead of expressing those ideas through traditional mediums, I explore them through code.

Creative coding allows me to translate experiences into interactive visuals. Even simple sketches can represent curiosity, experimentation, or an idea I want to explore further.

## What My Work Expresses

The art I create through code is often a reflection of who I am and what interests me. It mirrors the things I enjoy exploring, the patterns I find interesting, and the experiences that leave an impression on me.

Creative coding gives me a way to combine technical thinking with personal expression. It allows me to build something functional while also creating something visual and expressive.

In this sense, the sketches become small reflections of my interests and personality.

## Art as Expression and Relief

I believe art is a powerful form of expression. It allows people to communicate ideas, emotions, and perspectives that may be difficult to describe in words.

For me, creating art through code is also a form of relief. It provides a space to experiment, explore, and create without rigid constraints.

The interactive nature of creative coding makes this process even more engaging because the work responds dynamically to the viewer or the environment.

## What I Want to Build for Others

One of the aspects that excites me about the Processing ecosystem is that it enables people from many different backgrounds to explore programming creatively.

I want to contribute to tools that make this exploration easier and more enjoyable for beginners and creators.

Improving developer tools, debugging features, and user experience in environments like the p5.js Web Editor can help learners experiment more confidently and better understand how their code affects visual output.

By improving these tools, contributors can help thousands of learners explore creative coding more effectively.

## What Kind of Creator I Am

I see myself as someone who enjoys building things and improving how people interact with tools.

I am particularly interested in enhancing user experience and making tools easier to understand and use. When software becomes more intuitive, it allows users to focus more on creativity and exploration instead of struggling with technical barriers.

As a creator, I am motivated by both the process of building and the opportunity to help others create.

Through open source contributions and creative coding, I hope to support learners, artists, and developers who want to explore programming as a creative medium.
